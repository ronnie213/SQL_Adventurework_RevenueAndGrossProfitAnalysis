## <p align="center"> 🚲 Adventurework QoQ Revenue and Gross Profit Analysis 🚲 </p>
*This project analyses the root causes of the latest quarterly results’ variances using SQL and recommendations for management to improve profitability.*

### 📊 Analysis Overview
*1. Executive Summary \
2. By quarter, subcategory, region and channel \
3. Insights & Recommendations \
4. Limitations \
5. Follow-up actions*

### 📋 Executive Summary
#### *Revenue has fallen QoQ by $1.6m mainly due to*

- *$1.0m contributed by lower QoQ average selling price (ASP) and lower QoQ quantity sold for Road Bikes and Mountain Bikes,*
- *$0.8m contributed by lower QoQ quantity sold offset by slightly higher QoQ ASP.*

#### *Further analysis on the top 3 contributors showed*
*US market is the main contributor for the fluctuations which were driven by*
- *Lower ASP and lower sales volume from the Value-added reseller (VAR) channel for Mountain and Road Bikes,*
- *Lower ASP and lower sales volume from VAR channel coupled with lower sales volume from Specialty Bike Shope (SBS) channel offset by higher ASP.*

#### *Gross profits however, have increased QoQ by $11.8k due to higher QoQ gross margins*
- *$32.7k from Touring Bikes from lower negative margins offset by slightly lower volume sold,*
- *$15.5k from higher sales volume from Road Frames, Pedals, Mountain Frames, Handlebars and Helmets at comparable margins,* 
*offset by,*
- *$30.3k from Mountain bikes due to lower volume sold offset by higher margins,*
- *$6.0k from lower volume of Cranksets, Shorts sold.*

#### *On the huge drop in GP contributed by Mountain Bikes,*
- *The United States market contributed most to the drop.*
- *Analysing by channels also showed that lowering prices showed limited effectiveness in increasing sales volume for Mountain Bike.*
- *Further analysis on the US market for Mountain Bikes supported this. Lowering prices proves to be effective only in the VAR channel and WH channel in Q3.*

### 🎯 Insights & Recommendations
- *For the VAR channel in US, we should price Mountain Bikes in the range of $750 - $1,000 to increase gross profit as data showed lower prices drive sales volume consistently.*
- *For the WH channel in US, Mountain Bikes should be priced at a range of around $800 - $1,100 in Q3 to drive higher sales volume and gross profit. For the rest of the year, reduction in prices is not recommended due to weak correlation in prices and demand to maintain profitability.*
- *For the SBS channel in US, Mountain Bikes should be priced in the range of $1,250 - $1,375 to maintain profitability since lower prices do not translate into higher sales volume.*
- *Additionally, management should also consider exiting the SBS channel in the US market given the small size and declining sales volume despite lowered prices to reallocate resources to other regions/channels.*

### ⚠️ Limitations of analysis
- *Given the limited amount of data points, some of these data points may be skewed by one-time occurrence effects.*
- *More data points are required to better understand the impact on seasonality demand if any.*
- *More information on the cost structure for each channel is also required to analyse the GPM trends better and isolate impact of ASP on gross profits.*
- *More information on competitors’ pricing strategy required for a more effective pricing strategy.*
